Fallout Content

Welcome to my page where I focus entirely on the Fallout franchise and the misery the lore has brought into my life. I have spent far too long in this fanbase to not have opinions, ideas, and excessive knowledge on the backstory.

I ALSO know I'm not the only one thrilled to hear that the next Fallout game will be taking place somewhere outside of the USA! I know it's an alternate universe and all, but I absolutely have theories on what's coming next in the series based on things we learn in some of the more recent installments. But we can get back to that later! For now, let's go over some of the Easter eggs you can find in Fallout if you're willing to do a bit of exploring. I won't be going into any that are really valuable just yet as I'd like to spend more time on those and give instructions on how to find them. :)

Images for Fallout Content

If you've ever read The Cask of Amontillado, this one is probably going to give you a laugh, but it's actually referring to a bit of history! In Fallout 4, you're able to find an old, run down castle. This is actually Fort Independence in Boston, the place where Edgar Allen Poe initially heard a story that gave him the inspiration to write his tale. At the time, he was stationed there as a private. Therefore, in the basement of Fort Independence in game, you can find a few bottles of wine labelled as Amontillado, and a man built into a brick wall. Macabre, but VERY cool if you ask me.

This one is for any Dr. Who fans out there. In Fallout New Vegas, you can find an NCR Emergency Radio broadcast that makes a reference to the fine doctor himself and the network its played on. The line in question is "Bravo Bravo Charlie, the Doctor is coming." You might also find that some Securitrons you have to take out yell "EXTERMINATE" while shooting at you. Here's looking at you, Daleks.

Finally, Elvis Presley. No, I'm not kidding. The group "The Kings" in Fallout New Vegas is a group of Elvis impersonators, but... it's been 200 years since the war, so they have NO idea who Elvis is, and they also have no clue about his nickname. All they know is that, as The Kings, they feel they need to take care of the local people in an honorable fashion. They found some old notes, they never exactly say HOW they found these notes, but the notes taught them how to dress down to the way they slick back their hair, and how to talk.

Indie Games

AAA game studios don't always get it right, from the way Bethesda monetized Fallout 76, to the absolute flop that was Blizzard removing Overwatch from the libraries of people who owned it and releasing the new pay-to-play Overwatch 2, it seems there is always something to be desired. Indie game developers create amazing stories with minimal resources and usually sell their games for much cheaper prices.

Despite how much I love my Bethesda games, it's incredibly important to support indie developers and their craft, not just because they deserve it, but also to push for better quality work in AAA companies. The best way to remind these companies of the quality we as customers are looking for is to back projects that are striving for that quality.

With that being said, let's discuss some indie games in the current sphere that paved the way for future developers.

Images for Indie Games

First of all, it would be a mistake to skip talking about Five Night's At Freddy's. Love it or hate it, it opened the door for THOUSANDS of indie developers to start being recognized for their work.

Scott Cawthon was a struggling developer before he began work on the FNAF series. He developed games, but always got less than pleased feedback. After 20 years of making games, he was ready to throw in the towel, but decided to take one last stab at making games by using the negative criticism he'd gotten about his character designs to make something MEANT to be scary. Today, almost 10 years after the release of the first game, the franchise is still one of the biggest success stories in indie development.

Only a year later, the game Undertale made by indie developer Toby Fox would release, also to critical acclaim. Both of these indie games turned into HUGE successes in a relatively short period of time, paving the way for other indie developers to begin to show their work.

In more recent works by indie developers, you might recognize names like Hollow Knight, Among Us, Stardew Valley, or Celeste. You might even recognize the best selling video game in the world...

I say all of this to say I think that indie developers NEED to be encouraged and acknowledged, and as I add more entries to this site, I intend to spend a lot of time talking about indie developers and their work. The amazing things a small team or even one person can do deserve recognition!
